# How to Submit a Problem

This guide walks you through submitting a problem to the AI4Change Problem Board. A well-written problem description helps the community understand the issue and propose effective solutions.

## Step 1: Check for Existing Problems

Before submitting, search the [existing issues](https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues) to see if someone has already raised this problem. If a similar problem exists:

- **Vote on it** — click the thumbs-up (👍) reaction to show support
- **Add context** — comment with your local perspective, data, or experience
- **Don't duplicate** — duplicates split community attention and slow things down

## Step 2: Open the Problem Template

Go to **[Submit a Problem](https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues/new?template=problem.yml)** to open the issue form. The template guides you through the key sections.

## Step 3: Write a Clear Title

Your title should describe the problem in one line, starting with `[Problem]:`.

### Good Titles

- `[Problem]: Rural schools in Kenya lack electricity for computer labs`
- `[Problem]: Farmers in Bangladesh can't predict flood timing for crop planting`
- `[Problem]: No mental health services exist within 200km of refugee camps in Uganda`

### Bad Titles

- `Education is broken` — too vague, not actionable
- `We need an app` — describes a solution, not a problem
- `Help` — not descriptive at all
- `AI for Africa` — too broad, no specific problem

## Step 4: Describe the Problem

This is the most important section. A great problem description answers these questions:

### What's happening?

Describe the situation concretely. Use numbers and specifics where possible.

> **Good**: "Rural communities in the Sundarbans delta region of Bangladesh rely on tube wells that are frequently contaminated with arsenic. Testing costs $5-8 per sample and takes 2-3 weeks. Most families earn $2-3/day and can't afford regular testing."
>
> **Weak**: "Water quality is bad in some places."

### Who is affected?

Be specific about the population, scale, and how they're impacted.

> **Good**: "35+ million people in Bangladesh are exposed to arsenic levels above WHO guidelines. Women and children are disproportionately affected."
>
> **Weak**: "A lot of people."

### Where is it happening?

Include the country, region, and specific areas if possible. This helps the community understand the context and find local partners.

> **Good**: "Khulna and Satkhira districts, Sundarbans delta region, southwestern Bangladesh."
>
> **Weak**: "Asia."

### What's been tried?

Explain what solutions have been attempted and why they haven't worked. This prevents the community from reinventing the wheel and helps focus on what's actually needed.

> **Good**: "Government tube well testing exists but results are painted on well platforms and aren't updated. NGO filter programs have distributed filters, but maintenance is difficult and replacement parts aren't locally available."
>
> **Weak**: "Nothing works."

## Step 5: Suggest How AI Could Help

You don't need to be a technical expert. Just describe what a solution might look like from the user's perspective. The community will figure out the technical details.

Some prompts to get you thinking:

- What information would help people make better decisions?
- What pattern could a computer detect that humans miss?
- What repetitive task could be automated to free up human time?
- What data exists that isn't being used?

## Step 6: Add Location Context

Location matters for AI4Change problems. Include:

- **Country and region**: The specific area where the problem is most acute
- **Scale**: Is this one village, a district, a country, or a global pattern?
- **Urban/Rural**: The setting affects what technology is available
- **Connectivity**: Do people have smartphones? Internet? Feature phones only?

## Step 7: Submit and Engage

After submitting:

1. **Watch the issue** — you'll get notified when people comment
2. **Respond to questions** — the community may ask for more context
3. **Share the issue** — spread the word in relevant communities
4. **Vote on other problems** — help prioritize the board by voting (👍) on issues that matter to you

## How Voting Works

Every issue has a **reactions** bar at the top. Click the **👍 (thumbs up)** to vote for a problem. The more votes a problem gets, the higher it rises in the community's priority list.

- **5+ votes**: The issue gets the 🔥 `trending` label automatically
- **Trending issues**: Get more visibility and attract solution proposals
- **High-vote issues**: Are candidates for AI4Change to commit to building

You can also use other reactions to express your feelings:
- 👍 = I want this solved
- ❤️ = This resonates with me personally
- 🚀 = I'd contribute to building this
- 👀 = I'm watching this with interest

## Tips for Great Submissions

1. **Be specific, not generic** — "malaria prediction in rural Mozambique" beats "healthcare in Africa"
2. **Include numbers** — how many people, what cost, what time wasted
3. **Describe the human impact** — help people understand why this matters
4. **Mention what data exists** — open datasets, satellite imagery, government records
5. **Name potential local partners** — NGOs, universities, government agencies working in this space
6. **Keep it focused** — one problem per issue, even if problems are related
7. **Write in any language** — if you're more comfortable in another language, submit in that language and the community will help translate

## Example: A Good Submission

Here's a real seed problem from the board that demonstrates all the best practices:

> **Title**: [Problem]: Crop disease detection for smallholder farmers in East Africa
>
> **Problem**: Smallholder farmers lose 20-40% of crops to diseases because they can't identify infections until it's too late. The nearest agricultural extension officer is 50+ km away, and there's 1 officer per 3,000 farmers.
>
> **Who**: 33 million farming households across Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda. Average farm is 0.5-2 hectares. Women manage 60-80% of food production.
>
> **Where**: Western Kenya, Lake Zone Tanzania, Central Uganda.
>
> **Tried**: Government extension services (underfunded), NGO training (doesn't scale), PlantVillage app (requires internet, English-only).
>
> **AI idea**: Offline-first phone app that identifies crop diseases from camera photos, gives treatment advice in Swahili/Luganda/Kinyarwanda.

See this and other examples at: [Browse seed problems →](https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues?q=is%3Aissue+is%3Aopen+label%3Aseed-problem)
